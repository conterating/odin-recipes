Apple Pie Filling

Description

Creating a delicious apple pie filling is a wonderful way to capture the essence of autumn in a dessert. Begin by selecting a variety of apples known for their firm texture and balanced flavor, such as Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or Braeburn. Peel, core, and slice the apples into uniform pieces, about half an inch thick. In a large bowl, toss the apple slices with a mixture of sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of salt. This seasoning blend enhances the natural sweetness of the apples while adding warm, aromatic notes to the filling.

Ingredients

  • Apples
  • Lemon Juice
  • Water
  • Sugar
  • Cornstarch
  • Salt
  • Spices

Steps

  1. Place apples in a bowl. Toss apples with lemon juice in a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Pour water into a Dutch oven over medium heat. Combine sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, salt, and nutmeg in a bowl; add to water, stir well, and bring to a boil. Boil for 2 minutes, constantly stirring.
  3. Add apples and return to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until apples are tender, 6 to 8 minutes. Cool for 30 minutes.
  4. Ladle into 5 freezer containers, leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Cool at room temperature no longer than 1 1/2 hours.
  5. Seal and freeze. Can be stored for up to 12 months.
  6. Enjoy!
